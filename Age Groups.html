Age Groups

TNTT consists of 4 different age groups. Each age group is represented by a different color.

Au Nhi, ages 7-9

Ideal: The Child Jesus, the obedient Son to Mary and Joseph Teaching Method: Youth leaders hold complete authority and control of the flow of content. Model just, empathic, and ethical behavior.

  • Blank slate, seeker of knowledge (be careful of what youth leaders say and do in front of Seedlings)
  • Short attention span (keep lessons short and interesting)
  • Start to develop self-concepts, ego-centered (promote and encourage sharing, embrace mistakes which can teach Seedlings courage, wisdom, and alternatives – growth mindset)
  • Gender and racial identity development
  • Positive and negative reinforcements dictate morality
  • Play is work (provide opportunities for games)

Thieu Nhi, ages 10-12

Ideal: Jesus, the young Son at Nazareth Teaching Method: Youth leaders hold the most authority (teacher‐centered) and demonstrate or model appropriate behavior. Assign tasks.

  • Friendships based on trust and commonality
  • Gender separation
  • Follow social norms
  • Self-esteem, skill awareness
  • Willing to learn and listen (assign projects, reporting tasks)
  • Emerging critical thinking skills with a capacity for abstraction and logic

Nghia Si, ages 13-15

Ideal: Jesus, the One proclaiming the Good News Teaching Method: Youth leaders facilitate learning and interaction among Companions, and should give them a more perceived freedom. Activities are more social and group-oriented in nature

  • Continue to build friendship
  • Formation of cliques
  • Seek for popularity
  • Self-concept, appearance is important
  • Social relationship determines roles
  • Prone to peer pressure and conformity

Hiep Si, ages 16-17

Ideal: Jesus, the One proclaiming the Good News with a focus on the Eight Beatitudes Teaching Method: Youth leaders hold little authority in the control of the flow of content. The Knights should have more group discussions and assignments, community projects, etc. This age is a time of intense emotions and relationships with peers and family. The following points outline essential psychological developments common to the Knight’s age group and offer effective approaches to working with them. These points will address the cause and reaction to the Knight’s sense of independence, individual identity, and social fit.
